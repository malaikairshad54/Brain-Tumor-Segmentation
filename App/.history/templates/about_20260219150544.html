About NeuroSeg AI

Understanding our brain tumor detection technology

What is Brain Tumor Segmentation?

Brain tumor segmentation is the process of identifying and delineating tumor regions in medical images, specifically MRI scans. This is crucial for diagnosis, treatment planning, and monitoring disease progression. Manual segmentation is time-consuming and subject to inter-observer variability, making automated solutions essential.

Our AI Technology

We utilize a U-Net convolutional neural network architecture, which is specifically designed for biomedical image segmentation tasks. The model was trained on the BraTS (Brain Tumor Segmentation) dataset, achieving a Dice coefficient of 0.78, indicating high accuracy in tumor boundary detection.

MRI Modalities Used

Our system processes four complementary MRI sequences: FLAIR, T1-weighted, T1-weighted with contrast enhancement (T1ce), and T2-weighted images. Each modality provides unique information about different tissue characteristics, enabling comprehensive tumor analysis.

Performance Metrics

The model achieves 78% Dice similarity coefficient, processes images in under 3 seconds, and provides quantitative tumor burden assessment. The segmentation results are visualized with color overlays for easy interpretation by medical professionals.

Clinical Applications

This technology assists radiologists in tumor detection, helps plan surgical interventions, monitors treatment response, and supports research in neuro-oncology. It serves as a valuable second opinion tool in clinical decision-making processes.

Technical Implementation

Built with PyTorch and Flask, the application features a web-based interface for easy access. The backend handles image preprocessing, model inference, and result visualization. The system is designed for scalability and can be integrated into existing hospital workflows.

Technical Details

Model Architecture

U-Net with encoder-decoder structure, optimized for 2D brain MRI segmentation

Training Dataset

BraTS 2020 dataset with multi-institutional, multi-modal MRI data

Input Resolution

192x192 pixels per slice, supporting both 2D and 3D NIfTI formats

Output Format

Binary segmentation masks with tumor probability maps
